About Hinamatsuri

Japanese Girls' Festival

Hinamatsuri, also called Doll's Day or Girls' Day, is a special celebration of Japanese culture honoring daughters and their happiness, health, and growth. Join us for a day of traditional performances, crafts, and cultural experiences.

This reimagined Hinamatsuri blends tradition with bold modernity, celebrating Japanese culture through the lens of contemporary womanhood. Alongside classic elements like Hina dolls, tea ceremonies and Japanese food, we explore identity, empowerment and cultural transformation through interactive art and workshops.

Traditionally, families display Hina dolls symbolising prayers for daughters' happiness and prosperity. Dating back to the Heian period, this thousand-year-old festival coincides with peach blossoms—symbols of renewal and beauty. While honouring these customs, we create space for reflection on a central question: "What does happiness mean for girls and women today?"

Timed near International Women's Day, our celebration sparks intergenerational dialogue, honouring the past while amplifying voices shaping the future. For Japanese-Australians, it strengthens cultural connections; for the wider community, it's an engaging gateway into Japanese heritage and craftsmanship.

Join us in transforming Girls' Day into a vibrant cultural exploration that welcomes all genders and celebrates the evolving voices of multicultural women and girls in Australia today.

The Meaning of Hinamatsuri Foods

Each traditional food served during Hinamatsuri carries special meaning. The colorful hina-arare represents the four seasons. Clam soup symbolizes a loving couple, as paired shells fit only with each other. The diamond-shaped hishi mochi's three colors represent snow (white), peach blossoms (pink), and new growth (green).

🍱

Onigiri, the beloved Japanese rice ball, represents sustenance and care. Traditionally made by hand and shared with love, these simple yet nourishing treats embody the spirit of togetherness and the comfort of home cooking that is central to Japanese family life.

Our Mission

Japanese Girls' Day Reimagined is more than a cultural festival. We are committed to women and girls empowerment, creating spaces where everyone can thrive regardless of gender, background, or circumstances. We celebrate diversity, champion equality, and build vibrant, inclusive communities.

Through cultural education, community engagement, and advocacy, we work to dismantle barriers and create opportunities for all women and girls to reach their full potential.

About Us

Flowwa is a grassroots initiative championing women and girls' empowerment, diversity, equality, and community building. We believe in creating inclusive spaces where everyone can flourish, regardless of gender, background, or circumstances.

Our work centers on breaking down barriers and opening doors of opportunity. Through cultural celebrations, educational programs, mentorship initiatives, and community engagement, we foster environments where women and girls can develop their full potential.

We celebrate the rich diversity of our communities, honoring different cultures, traditions, and perspectives while working toward a more equitable future. The Japanese Girls' Festival - Reimagined exemplifies our mission: taking a beautiful cultural tradition and using it as a platform to promote empowerment, education, and unity.

At Flowwa, we're not just organizing events - we're building movements. We're creating networks of support, spaces for growth, and opportunities for leadership. We're committed to ensuring that every woman and girl has the resources, encouragement, and community backing to pursue their dreams and make their voices heard.

Join us in reimagining a world where equality, diversity, and empowerment are not just ideals, but lived realities for all.
